## Self Reflection (Success Factors Questions)

### Question 1

_How have you excelled in the last six months? Share 2-3 strengths you are leaning on for your areas of strongest performance. Consider what you delivered, and how you demonstrated MongoDB's [Leadership Commitment](https://help-hr.mongodb.com/hc/en-us/articles/15670365738899-The-MongoDB-Leadership-Commitment) in the delivery._

#### Strategic Execution Under Ambiguity

I demonstrated **Leaders are resourceful** and **Leaders focus on outcomes** by transforming ambiguous directives into measurable results. I coordinated with stakeholders to distill the docs org's evolving FY26 SMART goal into a trackable plan with monthly reporting, **achieving the 1000 examples goal on schedule**. On the sample app initiative, I aligned with Charlie Xu's Growth team while balancing priorities across three stakeholder groups, delivering 1 production app with Next.js frontend and 3 backends in November that **achieved 964 clones, 1,592 unique views, 7 forks, and 3 stars within 6 weeks**.

#### Scaling Impact Through Delegation and Advising

I maximized impact by **developing my people** and delegating strategically. For Grove and code examples audit tooling, I shaped early direction and built prototypes, then enabled Dachary Carey to lead execution while I advised and championed at the Leads level—demonstrating **Leaders attract and retain great people**. I applied this during the App Services EOL, managing end-to-end deprecation while delegating work and maintaining stakeholder alignment, **achieving a clean EOL in September with zero major customer-facing gaps** and demonstrating **Leaders are obsessed with their customers**.

#### Innovation and Technical Leadership

I drove innovation beyond core responsibilities. Dachary Carey and I co-created Ask CAL, a Skunkworks project enabling chatbot interaction with docs code examples. I designed the architecture and built the frontend, demonstrating **Leaders never stop learning and growing**—**Ask CAL was selected as 1 of only 14 finalists from 160 Skunkworks 2025 submissions**. When the monorepo migration caused widespread Git issues, I created and now lead a cross-team working group to modernize standards and unblock 50+ writers and leads, demonstrating **Leaders embrace adversity** by turning a systemic problem into an opportunity to strengthen team capabilities.
